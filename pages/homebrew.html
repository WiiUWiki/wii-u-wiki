What is homebrew?

When it comes to video games, homebrew is any unofficial software accessible through some sort of method or exploit for closed/non-user-programmable video game systems. Performing such methods or exploits to a video game console is known as homebrewing, modding, jailbreaking, or hacking. With a homebrewed/modded/jailbroken/hacked Wii U, or a Wii U with Custom Firmware (CFW for short) installed, almost all barriers break.

Ok... But what can you do with homebrew on the Wii U?

With homebrew, you can...

  • Use emulators on your console.
  • Play GameCube games.
  • Run backups of Wii and Wii U games from an SD card or hard drive.
  • Play region-locked games from other countries.
  • Use homebrew software.
  • Install and play mods for games.
  • And more!

With that being said, because of the power that homebrewing gives you to your console, it can also be used for malicious intent such as installing pirated games and/or using cheats to cheat online. We heavily encourage you to not use homebrew for these kind of purposes.

Could homebrew cause damage/break your Wii U?

Yes-ish. There is always very small possible chance that your console could brick if anything you do is done improperly, you are messing with something that you shouldn't be messing with, or you're doing something but don't know what you're doing. A bricked console is the equivalent of a console in a complete unusable state. In other words, Bricking a console is basically breaking it in a way that it fails to boot correctly. While there are ways to fix a brick, bricks can vary depending on what caused it, and for the most part you will need extra hardware or a hardmod on your Wii U to fix a brick. However, don't worry. As long as you carefully pay attention to this guide and know what you're doing, for which you are responsible for your actions, you shouldn't experience a brick.
